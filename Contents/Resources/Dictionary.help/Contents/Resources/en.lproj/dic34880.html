Dictionary Help

Use the Dictionary app to see definitions of words and phrases from a variety of sources.

Tip: You can also quickly look up words while you’re working in an app or browsing webpages.

You can open Dictionary from Launchpad (click the Launchpad icon in the Dock).

Open Dictionary for me

Search for a word or phrase in Dictionary

  • Type a word or phrase in the search field in the upper-right corner of the Dictionary window.

    If you look up a word in a definition, a SnapBack button appears in the search field; click it to return to your starting definition. You can also use the arrow buttons in the toolbar of the Dictionary window to move between definitions you viewed.

Make text bigger or smaller

  • Click the “A” buttons in the toolbar of the Dictionary window as many times as needed.

    The font stays at the new size until you click an “A” button again.

Customize Dictionary sources

  • In Dictionary, choose Dictionary > Preferences. You can select and reorder sources, such as Spanish or Korean dictionaries. You can set options for a source, such as how to display pronunciations, or which language versions of Wikipedia to search. The sources you select here determine the sources shown in Dictionary and when you look up words.

Hide profanity

  • If you’re an administrator of a Mac, you can control other users’ access to inappropriate words. Just set up parental controls on the Mac, then select the Hide profanity in Dictionary checkbox in the Other pane of Parental Controls preferences. Once set, only an administrator can remove parental controls.

If you search for a word and Dictionary can’t find any results, the word may not be in the sources selected in Dictionary preferences, or parental controls may be set. If possible, Dictionary suggests alternative words.

You can also use Spotlight to get a quick definition. Click the magnifying glass icon in the upper-right corner of the screen, then type a word or phrase.
